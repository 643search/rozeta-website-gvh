How to Choose an AI Consultant for Your Small Business

Before you sign a contract or join any program, run every candidate through these five filters:

  • 1
    Have they worked with businesses your size?

    Ask to see case studies from companies with 5–50 employees and revenue under $10M. A consultant who primarily serves enterprise clients will apply enterprise thinking to your SMB problem — and it won't fit. Rozeta Labs' entire practice is built for operators at this scale.

  • 2
    Do they train your team, or just you?

    The single biggest failure mode in SMB AI adoption: only the owner learns. When they leave a meeting, nothing changes. The right consultant builds capacity across your whole team. According to MIT research, team-wide AI adoption generates 3–5x the productivity gains of individual adoption.

  • 3
    Do they build systems or just give advice?

    Advice is cheap. What you need is deployed systems: the AI follow-up sequence actually running in your CRM, the proposal template actually integrated into your workflow. Ask: "What will be operational in my business after working with you?" If the answer is vague, walk away.

  • 4
    Is pricing transparent and accessible?

    Legitimate SMB-focused AI consultants don't hide pricing behind "contact us for a quote." You should be able to understand what you're paying and what you're getting before any sales call. Beware firms that require large upfront commitments for discovery work.

  • 5
    Is there ongoing support after implementation?

    AI tools change monthly. A one-time engagement that delivers a static playbook will be outdated within a year. The best AI consulting relationships include an ongoing community or advisory component so your team keeps pace with the technology. This is the core differentiator of Rozeta Labs' community model.

  • How much does AI consulting cost for a small business?

    Costs vary significantly by model. Freelance AI consultants charge $75–$250/hr. Community-based programs like Rozeta Labs offer ongoing membership at accessible monthly rates. Project-based boutique consulting for custom implementation typically runs $5,000–$30,000 depending on scope.   • Is AI consulting worth it for a small business?

    Yes — when executed correctly. McKinsey research shows companies that adopt AI systematically see 20–30% productivity gains in affected functions. For service businesses, the fastest ROI comes from automating customer follow-up (recovering lost leads), generating proposals faster, and reducing admin time. The key is finding a consultant who focuses on operational deployment, not tool demos. A well-implemented AI follow-up sequence alone can recover 10–20% of leads that would otherwise go cold.

  • How long does AI implementation take for a small business?

    Basic AI workflows — automated follow-ups, templated responses, scheduling bots — can be deployed in 2–4 weeks. Team-wide adoption of core tools typically takes 60–90 days with active training. Full operational transformation, where AI is embedded across sales, operations, and customer service, typically takes 3–6 months with an engaged implementation partner. Plan for iteration: the first version rarely survives contact with real workflows unchanged.

  • What ROI should I expect from AI consulting?

    Gartner research indicates AI-enabled companies outperform peers on revenue growth by 15–20% on average. For SMBs specifically, the most common ROI drivers are: reduced admin time (5–10 hrs/week per employee), faster lead response improving close rates, and proposal generation at 3–5x previous speed. Conservative expectations for a properly implemented SMB AI program: 10–15% improvement in operational efficiency within 90 days, with compounding gains as adoption deepens.

  • What's the difference between AI consulting and AI courses?

    AI courses teach concepts; AI consulting deploys systems. A course might explain how ChatGPT works — consulting builds the follow-up automation running in your CRM. The most effective model for SMBs combines both: community-based training that pairs education with hands-on implementation, so you learn and build simultaneously. This hybrid is exactly what Rozeta Labs delivers — and why generic online courses rarely produce operational change in a business context.

  • What is a fractional AI officer, and do I need one?

    A fractional AI officer is an AI strategy expert who works part-time (typically 10–20 hours/month) for a company — owning AI adoption strategy without the cost of a full-time senior hire. For most SMBs with under $5M revenue, a fractional AI officer is overkill; a focused consulting engagement plus ongoing community membership delivers the same result at lower cost. Fractional AI officers become valuable when you have 3+ active AI projects running simultaneously and need dedicated strategic oversight. Rozeta Labs' consulting service functions as a fractional AI officer for most SMB clients.

  • How do I find a good AI consultant for my business?

    Look for five things: (1) case studies from businesses your size, not just enterprise logos; (2) a focus on outcomes and deployed systems, not just tool recommendations; (3) team training capability — not just owner advisory; (4) transparent pricing without six-figure minimums; (5) ongoing support or community after the initial engagement ends. Red flags: consultants who open with tool demos before understanding your workflow, no published pricing, and no verifiable client results from companies your size.
